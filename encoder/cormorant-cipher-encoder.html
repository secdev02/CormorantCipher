How to apply changes

01
Enter your secret above and click Encode. All four outputs are generated in the browser — nothing is sent to a server.
02
Open cormorant-cipher.html in a text editor. Find var SECRET_BITS, var SECRET_TEXT, and var STEG_LABELS in the <script> block near the bottom and replace them with Steps 1–3.
03
For Step 4, find the six specimen <div> elements in the HTML body — #s1, #s2, #s3, #s-uc, #s-lc, #s-dg — and replace their inner content with the corresponding blocks. Keep the outer <div> tags intact.
04
Update the tech panel display strings: find THEMAGICWORDSARESQUEAMISHOSSIFRAGE and the KREEK… base32 value in the visible HTML and replace with your new values from Steps 2 and 3.
05
Make sure var TOKEN_BASE = "[INSERT CANARYTOKEN HERE]"; contains your DNS Canarytoken domain. This is independent of the secret.
06
Open the updated file in a browser. The live decoder panel at the bottom should display your new secret string. The bit-strip visualiser will update to reflect the new bit pattern.
